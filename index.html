Introduction

GAFAM or GAMAM stands for Google, Apple, Facebook or Meta, Amazon, and Microsoft. It refers to the five largest and most influential American technology companies that dominate the digital economy through their services, products, and market capitalization.

Google

Google is a multinational technology company best known for its dominant search engine, which helps users find information on the internet quickly and efficiently. Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University, Google has since expanded into various sectors, including cloud computing, artificial intelligence, advertising, and hardware. The company's mission is to "organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful." Over the years, Google has developed numerous popular services like Gmail, Google Maps, YouTube, and the Android operating system, making it one of the most influential tech giants in the world. Its parent company, Alphabet Inc., oversees its diverse ventures and innovations.

Amazon

Amazon is an American company founded by Jeff Bezos in 1994. Originally an online bookstore, it became the world's largest online retail platform. It sells products, digital content (movies, books, music), and also offers cloud computing services through AWS (Amazon Web Services). Amazon is also active in robotics, artificial intelligence, and voice assistants like Alexa.

Facebook/Meta

Facebook/Meta is a social networking service and website that allows users to create personal profiles, connect with friends and family, share photos, videos, and updates, join interest-based groups, and communicate through private messages or public posts. Founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and others at Harvard University, Facebook(meta) has grown into one of the largest social media platforms in the world. It enables people to stay connected, discover news and events, promote businesses or causes, and interact with a global community in real time across web and mobile devices.

Notice:

Mark Zuckerberg changed the name of "Facebook" to "Meta" on the date of 10/28/2021.

Apple

Apple is a multinational technology company that designs, manufactures, and markets consumer electronics, computer software, and online services. It is widely known for its innovative products such as the iPhone, iPad, Mac computers, Apple Watch, and AirPods. Apple also offers a range of digital services including the App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, Apple TV+, and Apple Pay. Founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, Apple has become one of the world’s most valuable and influential technology companies, recognized for its sleek design, strong brand identity, and integrated ecosystem.

Microsoft

Microsoft is a multinational technology company that develops, licenses, and sells computer software, consumer electronics, and personal computers. It is best known for its Windows operating system, Microsoft Office suite, and Internet Explorer and Edge web browsers. Microsoft also produces hardware products like the Xbox gaming console and Surface devices, and it offers cloud services through Microsoft Azure. Founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen, Microsoft is one of the world’s leading tech companies, playing a major role in both consumer and enterprise computing worldwide.
